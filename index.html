About Me

Hi, I'm Ruth, a sophomore at MIT studying EECS. I love programming and algorithms, especially applying them in practical ways. Although I've been interested in STEM from a young age, I began pursuing CS in earnest in high school through personal projects, algorithmic coding competitions, and school clubs. Since then, I've learned a lot, exploring new technologies and electrical engineering, which I enjoy because I get to work with my hands. When I'm not studying or making something, I'm probably birdwatching, drawing, or baking.

Currently, I'm working on an implementing an out-of-core LU decomposition in the Julia Lab. I hope to gain a better understanding of high perfomance computing and distributed algorithms through the research. I'm also learning more about autonomous machines through MIT's New Engineering Education Threads and by being a developer on Battlecode.

Skills: C++, Python, Java, Assembly, Minispec/Bluespec, SQL, HTML/CSS/JS, Julia, SciKitLearn, Tensorflow

Working on: distibuted computing, MATLAB

Completed coursework: Differential Equations, Linear Algebra, Discrete Math, Fundamentals of Programming (Python)

Current coursework: Systems and controls, algorithms

Awards: 3x American Invitational Mathematical Examination (AIME) Qualifier, USA Computing Olympiad (USACO) Platinum Qualifier

Bird-ID Discord Bot

❮ ❯

In the 2020-2022 Science Olympiad cycles, the event Ornithology had a cult following. Because there were no good ways to practice identifying birds at the time, a friend and I made Bird-ID Bot as a fun way to help student study for Ornithology. It has been used by over 1000 users and has been adapted into other ID event bots (Fossils, Rocks and Minerals), although I was not involved with the expansion.
